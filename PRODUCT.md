# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Foreshadow serves a short-term-rental operation across three roles, each in a different context:

- **Property managers / operations coordinators** — the primary daily users. They run turnovers, assign tasks, and watch reservations across many properties at once, usually on desktop, often under time pressure between back-to-back bookings.
- **Cleaners / field staff** — on the ground, mostly on a phone, completing cleaning forms and tasks during a turnover. They need glanceable status and large, reliable touch targets.
- **Property owners** — lighter-touch. They check status, performance, and the state of their own properties at a glance, and trust that the operation is handled.

## Product Purpose

Foreshadow is the **unified operations cockpit** for a short-term-rental business: one place where reservations (synced from Hostaway), turnovers, tasks/assignments, guest messages, and automations all live together. It exists so a manager never has to stitch together a PMS, a messaging app, a spreadsheet, and a group chat to know what needs to happen next.

Success is operational confidence: every turnover is covered by the right person at the right time, nothing falls through the cracks between reservations, and the team can see the whole operation without hunting for it.

## Brand Personality

**Calm, confident, trustworthy.** The interface is quiet by default and gets out of the way so work flows. It reads as a serious operations tool — composed under pressure, never noisy or anxious — while still feeling crafted and modern (the liquid-glass surfaces, the cohesive purple-on-cool palette). The voice is plain and direct: it tells you what's happening and what to do, without hype.

## Anti-references

This should **not** look like:

- **A generic SaaS dashboard** — no hero-metric template (big number + small label + gradient accent), no endless identical icon+heading+text card grids, no decorative gradients.
- **A cluttered legacy PMS** (Guesty-era property software) — not cramped, dense, or overwhelming; whitespace and hierarchy do the work.
- **A toy / consumer app** — not over-rounded, over-colorful, or playful at the expense of being taken seriously for daily operations.
- **Cold enterprise software** — not sterile, gray, or soulless; warmth comes from craft, motion, and the accent palette, not from corporate minimalism.

## Design Principles

1. **Operational calm.** The default state is quiet. Surface what needs attention now; let everything else recede. Color and motion are signals, not decoration.
2. **One cockpit, no stitching.** Reservations, turnovers, tasks, and messages belong in one coherent surface. Reduce the number of places a manager has to look.
3. **Glanceable status, then depth.** Every surface answers "what's the state?" at a glance, with detail one interaction away — never the reverse.
4. **Field-ready.** Anything a cleaner touches must work on a phone, one-handed, with large targets and high contrast. Desktop density and mobile clarity are both first-class, not one ported to the other.
5. **Crafted, not loud.** Premium feel is earned through precise spacing, real motion, and a cohesive palette — not through effects shouting for attention.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast**: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, placeholders held to the same body standard (no light-gray-on-tint).
- **Field / mobile-first touch**: comfortable tap targets and outdoor-readable contrast on any surface cleaners use on a phone.
- **Reduced motion is honored**: every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative, and motion is never the only way state is communicated.
